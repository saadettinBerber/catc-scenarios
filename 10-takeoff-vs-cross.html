Take-Off vs Cross or Enter

An aircraft is given Take-Off while another aircraft is given Cross or Enter on the same runway

TAKE-OFF vs CROSS / ENTER

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway Holding Point Surveillance Route

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

1 Cross or Enter from Holding Point on Same Runway

If DLH321 is given Take-Off and CHECKER1 is given Cross or Enter from a Holding Point on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: DLH321 given Take-Off while CHECKER1 is given Cross or Enter from a Holding Point on the same runway

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    Holding Point and Route are needed to determine if DLH321 is given Take-Off and CHECKER1 is given Cross or Enter from a Holding Point on the same runway but behind DLH321, in this case ✓ NO ALERT would be triggered but jet blast needs to be taken into account.

📋 Quick Reference

Situation Description Alert Exemption
1 Cross or Enter from Holding Point on same runway CONFLICT Cross/Enter position is behind Take-Off position (jet blast consideration)
