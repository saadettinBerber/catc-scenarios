Line-Up vs Line-Up

Two aircraft receive Line-Up clearances on the same runway

LINE-UP vs LINE-UP

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway Holding Point Surveillance

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

1 Same Holding Point

If AZA654 is given Line-Up and IBE987 is given Line-Up from the same holding point on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: Both aircraft at the same holding point on the same runway

2 Directly Opposite Holding Point

If AZA654 is given Line-Up and AFR123 is given Line-Up from the holding point directly opposite on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 2: Aircraft at directly opposite holding points on the same runway

3 Opposite End of Runway

If AZA654 or AFR123 or DLH321 is given Line-Up and KLM789 is given Line-Up from a holding point at the opposite end of the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 3: Aircraft at opposite ends of the same runway (matches PDF layout)

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    If a conditional Line-Up / Line-Up in sequence is given then ✓ NO ALERT is triggered in Situation 1 and Situation 2.
  • 2
    ✓ NO ALERT is triggered in Situation 1 if multiple Line-Up from the same holding point is authorised at the aerodrome.
  • 3
    At some aerodromes, Line-Up vs Line-Up may be permitted in certain weather conditions (Local Rule).

📋 Quick Reference

Situation Position Alert Exemption

2 Directly opposite HP CONFLICT Conditional / Sequential LUP
