Take-Off vs Land

An aircraft is given Take-Off while another aircraft is cleared to Land on the same or crossing/converging runways

TAKE-OFF vs LAND

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway/s & Mode of Operation Holding Point Surveillance

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

Single Runway

1 Take-Off from Holding Point, Landing on Same Runway

If KLM789 is given Take-Off from the holding point and IBE789 is cleared to Land on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: KLM789 given Take-Off from holding point while IBE789 is cleared to Land on the same runway

2 Lined Up on Runway, Landing on Same Runway

If DLH321 is given Take-Off and is lined up on the runway and IBE789 is cleared to Land on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 2: DLH321 given Take-Off whilst lined up, IBE789 cleared to Land on the same runway

3 Take-Off and Landing in Opposite Directions

If IBE987 is given Take-Off and AFR321 is cleared to Land on the same runway in the opposite direction.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 3: IBE987 given Take-Off while AFR321 is cleared to Land on the same runway in the opposite direction

Crossing/Converging Runways

4 Take-Off with Landing on Intersecting/Crossing Runway

If BAW456 is given Take-Off and is lined up on the runway and KLM987 is cleared to Land on an intersecting/crossing runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 4: BAW456 given Take-Off while KLM987 is cleared to Land on an intersecting runway

5 Take-Off with Landing on Converging Runway (Missed Approach Risk)

If BAW456 is given Take-Off and is lined up on the runway and DLH123 is cleared to Land on a converging runway (this alert is required in case the DLH123 performs a missed approach and could conflict with the departing BAW456).

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 5: BAW456 given Take-Off while DLH123 is cleared to Land on a converging runway (missed approach risk)

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    Local procedures may allow a landing Clearance to be given whilst the departing aircraft is still on the runway, this can be based on one or more of the following:
    • the positions of the aircraft.
    • the weather conditions.
    • a locally approved algorithm which predicts that the departing aircraft has crossed the end of the runway-in-use or has started a turn, when the arriving aircraft crosses the threshold.
    ✓ NO ALERT
