Land vs Take-Off

An aircraft is cleared to Land while another aircraft is given Take-Off on the same, crossing/converging, or parallel runways

LAND vs TAKE-OFF

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway/s Holding Point Surveillance

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

Single Runway

1 Landing and Take-Off on Same Runway

If AZA456 is given Cleared to Land and DLH321 is given Cleared to Take-Off on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: AZA456 cleared to Land while DLH321 is given Take-Off on the same runway

2 Landing and Take-Off in Opposite Directions on Same Runway

If AZA456 is given Cleared to Land and KLM789 is given Cleared to Take-Off on the same runway in the opposite direction.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 2: AZA456 cleared to Land while KLM789 is given Take-Off on the same runway in the opposite direction

Crossing/Converging Runways

3 Landing with Take-Off on Crossing Runway

If KLM987 is given Cleared to Land and BAW456 is given Cleared to Take-Off.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 3: KLM987 cleared to Land while BAW456 is given Take-Off on a crossing runway

4 Landing with Take-Off on Converging Runway (Missed Approach Risk)

If DLH123 is given Cleared to Land and BAW456 is given Cleared to Take-Off from a converging runway (this alert is required in case the DLH123 performs a missed approach and could conflict with the departing BAW456).

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 4: DLH123 cleared to Land while BAW456 is given Take-Off from a converging runway (missed approach risk)

Closely Spaced Parallel Runways

5 Landing with Take-Off on Adjacent Parallel Runway

At certain aerodromes with closely spaced parallel runways, local procedures may apply if FDX4 is given Cleared to Land and SAS1568 is given Cleared to Take-Off from the adjacent runway (this alert is required in case the FDX4 performs a missed approach it could conflict with the departing SAS1568 or the wake vortex from the FDX4 could interfere with the Take-Off run of the SAS1568).

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 5: FDX4 cleared to Land while SAS1568 is given Take-Off from an adjacent parallel runway (missed approach / wake vortex risk)

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    If LAHSO for KLM987 is in use then an alert is not triggered. ✓ NO ALERT
  • 2
    Local procedures may allow SAS1568 to be given Clearance to Take-Off if FDX4 is at a certain position in which case surveillance is needed to determine the position of the aircraft. ✓ NO ALERT

📋 Quick Reference
