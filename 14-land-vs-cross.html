Land vs Cross or Enter

An aircraft is cleared to Land while another aircraft/vehicle is given Cross or Enter on the same runway

LAND vs CROSS/ENTER

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway Holding Point Surveillance Route

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

1 Land with Cross (or Enter) on Same Runway

If IBE789 is given Cleared to Land and IBE987 is given Cross (or Enter) on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: IBE789 cleared to Land while IBE987 is given Cross (or Enter) on the same runway

2 Land with Enter (or Cross) on Same Runway

If KLM987 is given Cleared to Land and CHECKER1 is given Enter (or Cross) on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 2: KLM987 cleared to Land while CHECKER1 is given Enter (or Cross) on the same runway

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    Holding Point, Surveillance and Route are needed to determine if the position that CHECKER1 is Crossing or Entering from is behind the actual position of KLM987, in which case no alert is triggered. This allows the Controller to maintain a high runway throughput. ✓ NO ALERT
  • 2
    Local procedures may permit the situation where IBE789 (or KLM987) has landed and is still on the runway and is moving below a specified speed and is a certain distance from IBE987 (or CHECKER1) and the Controller has instructed IBE789 (or KLM987) to vacate at an exit before the crossing point of IBE987 (or CHECKER1). In this case surveillance, holding point and route are needed to determine whether to trigger an alert or not. ✓ NO ALERT
  • 3
    Local procedures may allow a Landing Clearance to be given whilst a mobile is crossing the same runway, this can be based on one or more of the following:
    • the positions of the mobiles.
    • the weather conditions.
    • a locally approved algorithm which predicts that the crossing mobile will be clear of the runway when the arriving aircraft crosses the runway threshold.
    ✓ NO ALERT

📋 Quick Reference

Situation Description Alert Exemption
1 Land with Cross (or Enter) on same runway CONFLICT Crossing behind landing aircraft; local procedures (speed, distance, exit)
2 Land with Enter (or Cross) on same runway CONFLICT Entering behind landing aircraft; local procedures (speed, distance, exit)
