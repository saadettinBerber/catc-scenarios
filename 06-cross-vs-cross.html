Cross or Enter vs Cross or Enter

Two aircraft or vehicles are given Cross or Enter clearances from opposite holding points on the same runway

CROSS / ENTER vs CROSS / ENTER

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway Holding Point Surveillance

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

1 Opposite Cross or Enter on Same Runway

If KLM789 (aircraft or vehicle) is given Cross or Enter and CHECKER1 (aircraft or vehicle) is given Cross or Enter from a holding point directly opposite on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: KLM789 and CHECKER1 given Cross or Enter from opposite holding points on the same runway

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    Surveillance is needed if Cross is given behind Enter to ensure that there is sufficient space for the mobile to Cross. ✓ NO ALERT
  • 2
    ✓ NO ALERT is triggered if both mobiles are vehicles (not aircraft).

📋 Quick Reference

Situation Description Alert Exemption
1 Opposite Cross/Enter on same runway CONFLICT Sufficient space behind Enter; both are vehicles
