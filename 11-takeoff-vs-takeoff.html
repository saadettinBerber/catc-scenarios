Take-Off vs Take-Off

An aircraft is given Take-Off while another aircraft is also given Take-Off on the same or crossing/converging runways

TAKE-OFF vs TAKE-OFF

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway/s Holding Point Surveillance

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

Single Runway

1 Second Aircraft Lined Up on Same Runway

If IBE987 is given Take-Off and BAW456 is given Take-Off whilst lined up on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: IBE987 given Take-Off while BAW456 is given Take-Off whilst lined up on the same runway

2 Second Aircraft at Holding Point, Same Direction

If IBE987 is given Take-Off and AFR123 is given Take-Off whilst at a holding point on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 2: IBE987 given Take-Off while AFR123 is given Take-Off from a holding point on the same runway

3 Second Aircraft at Holding Point, Opposite Direction

If IBE987 is given Take-Off and KLM789 is given Take-Off whilst at a holding point on the same runway in the opposite direction.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 3: IBE987 given Take-Off while KLM789 is given Take-Off from a holding point in the opposite direction

Crossing/Converging Runways

4 Take-Off from Intersecting Runway (Ground Trajectories Converging)

If IBE987 is given Take-Off and BAW456 is given Take-Off from a runway that intersects/crosses the runway being used by IBE987. When the aircraft ground trajectories are converging an alert is triggered.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 4: IBE987 and BAW456 given Take-Off from crossing runways with converging ground trajectories

5 Take-Off from Runway with Converging Climb-Out Trajectory

If BAW456 is given Take-Off and AFR123 is given Take-Off from a runway where the climb out trajectory converges with the runway being used by BAW456.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 5: BAW456 and AFR123 given Take-Off from runways with converging climb-out trajectories

Converging SIDs after Take-Off

6 Converging SID Routes from Different Runways

An alert is triggered if Aircraft A has Taken-Off and a Take-Off Clearance for Aircraft B from another runway is input within a defined time from the departure of Aircraft A, and the SID routes of both aircraft are known to conflict (this can be defined locally using look up tables).

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 6: Converging SID routes from different runways create a potential conflict point after departure

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    Local procedures may permit BAW456 to be given Take-Off before IBE987 is airborne, in which case surveillance is needed to determine the position of the aircraft. ✓ NO ALERT
  • 2
    Local procedures may permit AFR123 to be given Take-Off before BAW456 is airborne, in which case surveillance is needed to determine the position of the aircraft. ✓ NO ALERT
  • 3
    Surveillance data is used to determine whether one of the two aircraft has already passed a point on the runway that is considered as safe, after the crossing point of the runways, in which case ✓ NO ALERT is triggered.

📋 Quick Reference

Situation Description Alert Exemption
1 Both lined up on same runway CONFLICT Local procedures (surveillance needed)
2 Second at holding point, same direction CONFLICT Local procedures (surveillance needed)
3 Second at holding point, opposite direction CONFLICT Local procedures (surveillance needed)
4 Crossing runways, converging ground trajectories CONFLICT Aircraft passed safe point after crossing
5 Converging climb-out trajectories CONFLICT Aircraft passed safe point after crossing
6 Converging SIDs after Take-Off CONFLICT SID routes not known to conflict
