Land vs Land

An aircraft is cleared to Land while another aircraft is also cleared to Land on the same or crossing/converging runways

LAND vs LAND

📊 Data Required

Clearances Assigned Runway/s Holding Point Surveillance

⚠️ General Rule

Important: In each scenario, it is deemed that the first Clearance in the heading title is the one that has been input by the Controller first and the second Clearance triggers the alert.

🚨 Alert Triggered

Single Runway

1 Two Aircraft Cleared to Land on the Same Runway

If AZA456 is given Cleared to Land and IBE789 is given Cleared to Land on the same runway.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 1: AZA456 cleared to Land while IBE789 is also cleared to Land on the same runway

2 Two Aircraft Cleared to Land in Opposite Directions

If KLM987 is given Cleared to Land and DLH123 is given Cleared to Land on the same runway in the opposite direction.

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 2: KLM987 cleared to Land while DLH123 is also cleared to Land on the same runway in the opposite direction

Crossing/Converging Runways

3 Two Aircraft Cleared to Land with Converging Air Trajectories

If both KLM987 and DLH123 are given Cleared to Land and have converging air trajectories (this could be a local rule in case of both aircraft go around at the same time).

⚠ CONFLICT

Situation 3: KLM987 and DLH123 both cleared to Land with converging air trajectories

✅ Exemptions to the Rule

  • 1
    Local procedures may allow multiple landing Clearances to be given, this can be based on one or more of the following:
    • the positions of the aircraft.
    • the weather conditions.
    • A locally approved algorithm which predicts that the preceding aircraft will have vacated the runway when the following aircraft crosses the runway threshold.
    ✓ NO ALERT

📋 Quick Reference

Situation Description Alert Exemption
1 Two aircraft cleared to land on the same runway CONFLICT Local procedures (position, weather, algorithm)
2 Two aircraft cleared to land, opposite directions CONFLICT Local procedures (position, weather, algorithm)
3 Two aircraft cleared to land, converging trajectories CONFLICT Local procedures (position, weather, algorithm)
